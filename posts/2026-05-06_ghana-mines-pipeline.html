Ghana Mine Detection Pipeline

Project C · Progress Report — 2026-05-06

Project C
Ghana Mines
Project C — Galamsey (illegal artisanal gold mine) detection in Ghana. S2 imagery downloaded (70 tiles, 57 GB), Hansen forest-loss filtered (601k mining candidate patches), 79 mine site coordinates compiled. Preprocessing on Quest.
Author

Andrea Salem

Published

May 6, 2026

Superseded by Ghana Mines Stage 1: Pipeline + Baseline Results, which merges this pipeline report with training results and the six-workstream improvement plan.

1 Motivation

Ghana faces a severe illegal artisanal gold mining (Galamsey) crisis. Galamsey operations cause deforestation, mercury water contamination, and destruction of agricultural land. The problem is concentrated in the gold-bearing Birimian greenstone belts of southwest Ghana (Ashanti, Western, Eastern regions).

Goal: Reliably detect Galamsey mine sites from Sentinel-2 satellite imagery, then estimate their causal impact on local outcomes (health, water quality, economic activity).

Why this is tractable: Galamsey mines are spectrally distinctive — bare red soil and turbid water ponds carved into dense tropical forest create an extreme NDVI drop (~0.8 forest → ~0.2 mine). This is a stronger signal than the Tanzania mines that Christensen et al. (2025) detected at AUC 0.75.

Key references:

  • Christensen et al. (2025) NBER — Mine detection across Africa, AUC 0.75 on Tanzania, RCF method (GitHub unavailable)
  • Arezki et al. (2025) Oxford — AlphaEarth + CNN mine detection, presence only
  • Snapir et al. (2017) RSE — Mining deforestation in Ghana via Landsat NDVI time series

2 What Was Built

2.1 Task Scaffolding

Full task directory following the established repo pattern:

tasks/mines-ghana/
├── config.yaml          # task metadata + label provenance
├── README.md            # full documentation
└── labels/
    └── ghana_grid_halfdeg.shp  # 117 cells, 0.5° grid

Config entry minesGH-Ghana in config/config.yaml:

minesGH-Ghana:
  architecture: unet_tiny
  bands: [7, 1, 10, 11]  # NIR, Blue, SWIR2, NDVI
  patch_size: 64           # mines are 0.1–10 ha (10–32 px); 64-patch = mine fills ~25% vs ~6% at 128
  pos_weight: 15           # PLACEHOLDER — recalculate from actual class balance after preprocessing
  loss_function: focal
  hparam_overrides:
    learning_rate: 5.0e-6

NDVI bands were chosen based on the Rwanda buildings experience (Stage 2b: NDVI r=0.750 vs NIR r=0.432). NDVI is also the key discriminator for forest-to-mine transitions (Snapir et al. 2017).

patch_size was reduced from 128 to 64: Galamsey mines range from 0.1–10 ha (10–32 pixels at 10 m). In a 128×128 patch a 10 ha mine occupies ~6% of the area; in a 64×64 patch it occupies ~25%, giving the encoder denser positive signal. Preprocessing (0.5° grid tiling) is patch-size-independent — patch extraction happens at training time.

2.4 Hansen Filtering → Mining Candidate Patches

Applied spatial and morphological filters to separate mining-related deforestation from agricultural clearing:

Filter Positive pixels Patches
Raw forest loss (2015–2023, ≥30% canopy) 11,261,686 —
+ Gold belt restriction (Ashanti, Sefwi, Kibi) 8,118,056 —
+ Patch size filter (0.1–10.0 ha) 5,596,902 601,017

Filter rationale:

  • Gold belt restriction: Galamsey occurs in Birimian greenstone belts. Loss outside these zones is overwhelmingly agricultural.
  • Patch size 0.1–10 ha: Mine clearings are typically small and irregular. Agricultural clearings tend to be >10 ha and more regular.
  • Not yet applied: River proximity filter (alluvial gold clusters within 1 km of waterways). Would further reduce false positives.

Output: data/ghana/labels/gfw_mining_loss.shp (601,017 polygons) + gfw_mining_loss.tif (binary raster).

3 Label Strategy

Source Role Volume Precision
Hansen forest-loss (filtered) Primary training labels 601k patches 30 m (adequate for 128 px patches)
GPS mine coordinates Validation / hit-rate check 79 sites ~1–5 km (town centroids)
Ghana Minerals Commission Future validation (via Justina) TBD Unknown

The Hansen-based labels are “weak supervision” — they include some non-mining forest loss. This is acceptable for initial training because:

  1. The gold belt filter removes most agricultural clearing
  2. False positives in training data primarily affect recall, not precision
  3. The model will learn the mine spectral signature (bare soil + water in forest) regardless of label noise
  4. Validation against independent GPS sites provides a clean accuracy estimate

7 Key Design Decisions

Decision Rationale
Dry season composite (Nov–Feb) Minimal clouds; mine scars most visible (exposed bare soil)
NDVI bands (4-band input) Validated on Rwanda: NDVI r=0.750 vs NIR r=0.432; NDVI captures forest→mine contrast (Snapir 2017)
patch_size=64 (was 128) Mines are 0.1–10 ha = 10–32 px. 64-patch → mine fills ~25% vs ~6%. Denser positive signal for encoder.
Hansen as primary training labels Thousands of precise deforestation polygons vs 79 imprecise GPS points
Gold belt spatial filter Removes non-mining deforestation (agriculture, logging)
Patch size filter 0.1–10 ha Mine-scale clearings; excludes large agricultural blocks
Full Ghana AOI Allows detection of unknown sites, not just known hotspots
Processing on Quest HPC 57 GB imagery + GPU training exceed local disk/compute

8 Band Strategy

The current baseline uses S2 NDVI (4 bands). A ladder of follow-up experiments is planned after baseline results:

# Experiment Bands Status
0 S2 NDVI (baseline) 4 (NIR, Blue, SWIR2, NDVI) Current submission
1 +SWIR1 5 (+B11 for turbid pond separation) Planned
2 AE-16 (PCA) 16 (top PCA components from Alpha Earth) AE downloaded (314 GB) — report →
3 S2+AE-16 20 (S2 NDVI + AE-16) Planned
4 AE-64 (full) 64 (all Alpha Earth bands) Planned — needs larger architecture

Alpha Earth (64 bands) failed catastrophically for Rwanda buildings (district r = -0.29) because AE encodes land-cover semantics, not building presence. However, mines are a land-cover feature (forest → bare soil), so AE may perform better here. Arezki et al. (2025) used AE for mine detection with positive results.

For AE experiments, 64 bands is too high-dimensional for unet_tiny (~6,800 params). Band selection via PCA reduces to 16 bands. Script ready: src/preprocessing/10_band_selection_ae.py.
